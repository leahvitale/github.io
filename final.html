Principles of Wildlife Biology and Management (BIO 39AC) is a summer course offered by Metropolitan State University of Denver. This course covers topics such as the history of wildlife management, techniques used in wildlife biology & management, habitats, wildlife adaptations, management practices, wildlife interactions, and healthy communities and ecosystems.

There is a field portion of the course where students spend two weeks between Yellowstone National Park and Grand Teton National Park. The field portion of the course is an opportunity for students to learn from park employees and naturalist guides on how to apply the lecture material in real life. Students are also expected to make an observation in the field, and develop a research proposal from this observation. The last day of the course is dedicated to the groups going into the field to collect data for their research. Finally, students learn how to run statistics on their data and present their results. This course is specifically designed for students who anticipate finding a career as a wildlife biologist or zoologist.
